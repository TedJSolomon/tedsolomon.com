About Me

The longer story.

I didn't take the typical path into product. I think that's made me a better PM.

Background

"The best product people I've ever met didn't start in product."

I spent five years in heavy civil construction — first as an estimator, then as a project engineer. I built bid packages for complex infrastructure projects, coordinated field teams, managed subcontractor relationships, and got comfortable operating under real pressure with real stakes. Construction taught me to prioritize ruthlessly, communicate clearly, and make decisions with incomplete information. It turns out those skills translate well to product management.

In 2020, I made the jump to tech when I joined Beck Technology. I came in on the implementation side, working directly with enterprise customers — construction companies navigating digital transformation — as they adopted new estimating software. For three and a half years, I sat with users, watched them struggle, and translated their frustrations into the product feedback that actually gets things fixed. I built genuine relationships with the people whose jobs depended on getting this software right.

That experience gave me something most PMs don't have: I know what bad UX looks like when someone's livelihood is on the line. I know the difference between a feature request that sounds good in a meeting and one that would actually change someone's day. And I know how to earn trust with users who are skeptical of technology — because I used to be in their world.

In 2023, I transitioned into product management. Now I own the roadmap, drive discovery, and work with engineering to ship features that directly impact how construction companies estimate projects. I'm still in the room with customers as often as I can be. The domain knowledge doesn't hurt either.

Outside of work, I co-founded Vision Quest — a weekly tech newsletter for curious professionals — and I'm building Catalyft, a mobile goal-tracking app headed to the App Store. I believe the best products come from people who care enough to understand the problem before they try to solve it.
